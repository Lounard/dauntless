About

DAUNTLESS

Dauntless is a website that tackles about the different phobias children have. To start off, phobia is an extreme or irrational fear of or aversion to something, according to the online dictionary. The said website will also wander on the possible coping up methods or solutions to not further more increase the tendency to trigger these phobias. In addition, insert website name would also put in different hospital and institutions which aid persons having difficulty on handling their phobias. In relation, the said website will also discuss some certain issues in depression and anxiety because phobias can somehow be related to stress and depression. The main target users of the website are children whose age ranging from 7-13 because the publishers of the website concluded that it is better to solve the problem at an early age to prevent it from getting worse.

Different Types of Phobias

Social Phobia

These are phobias that involve fears in public or expressing your inner self in crowded places such as gerantophobia, gamophobia, and many more.

Agoraphobia

These are phobias that involve fears in natural occurrence or God-made phenomenon such as ablutophobia, lilapsophobia, ombrophobia, and the like.

Other Phobias

These are other fascinating phobias that consists of different topics on various phobias that you could not know you have.

I AM MENTALLY ILL, AND THIS IS MY STORY

It was a sunny Wednesday afternoon in 2014 when I learned that I had anxiety and depression. I went to see a psychologist at her clinic at UP Diliman. After an hour of sobbing and telling my story, she diagnosed me with anxiety and depression. And because my episodes had been going on for so long, she felt medications were necessary. She asked me to come back for another session, and she would then refer me to a psychiatrist. I thanked her, paid her secretary, and left her clinic.I did not come back for a follow-up, despite text messages from her secretary asking me when I would schedule my next visit.

Cognitive behavior therapy gave me the help that my medicines alone could not give. It taught me to overcome my negative, and oftentimes destructive, thinking patterns. My most destructive thought comes every time I feel insecure. I feel that I am not good enough, and that people around me are evaluating me using an invisible standard. I put certain people on a pedestal and think that I am nothing compared with them and with what they have accomplished.

This process helps me arrive at a positive and balanced thought. But sometimes, I do not succeed. There are days I am on a dip and I cannot do anything right. Nothing can lift my spirits. I just want to stay in bed all day and not move (literally!). I don’t even have the energy to eat. I don’t want to go out because I feel that the moment I step out of our front door, a series of unfortunate events is bound to greet me.

  • Psychotherapy

    Psychotherapy, also known as talk therapy, involves meeting with a therapist or other mental health professional on a regular basis.This gives you the opportunity to talk about your fears and any issues that may be contributing to your fears. Psychotherapy is often combined with medications for optimum effectiveness.

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)

    Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) is the most common form of psychotherapy used to treat people with phobias.CBT can help you understand the distorted feelings and views associated with phobias.It can also teach you how to work through stressful situations by replacing the distorted thoughts with healthy thoughts, allowing you to regain a sense of control in your life.
