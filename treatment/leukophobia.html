Treatment for Leukophobia

Exposure Therapy

Exposure therapy is a form of cognitive-behavioral therapy. It involves putting yourself into increasingly stressful scenarios involving your particular phobia and overcoming you fear with new learning.

Talk Therapy

Psychiatrists are qualified to work with leukophobia and some specialize in treating phobias. Generally though the process is slow and often the results are poor, because as dedicated and skilled as most psychiatrists are, their training does not include the most effective methods for dealing with serious fears.

Behavioural Therapy

Behavioural therapy involves one-to-one sessions with a therapist trained in treating phobias. The principle of this approach involves exposure and the gradual desentization to leukophobia.

Medications

Depending on your symptoms, your doctor may recommend medication to help reduce your anxiety and other leukophobia symptoms. These include:

Antidepressants called selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs)

Beta-blockers (Propranolol)

Sedative-hypnotic drugs (Xanax or Valium)

Lifestyle Changes

Lifestyle changes won’t necessarily treat leukophobia, but they may help reduce everyday anxiety. You may want to try:

Meditation Practice

Control Caffeine Consumption

Physical activity and exercise, which is known to manage the anxiety

Medicine can be prescribed, but please note that these medications can have side effects and/or withdrawal systems that can be severe. It is also important to note that medicines do not cure phobias, at best they only temporarily suppress the systems.
