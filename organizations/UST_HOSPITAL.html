USTH Background Information

The University of Santo Tomas Hospital is the third clinical hospital for the University of Santo Tomas. The university received its first teaching hospital on an 1875 order of King Alfonso of Spain, setting up instruction in the Franciscan hospital San Juan de Dios, established in 1577.

During the war, San Juan de Dios was converted to hold the Quezon Institute and St. Paul's Hospital given to the university, but the campus and hospital were destroyed in February 1945 during the Liberation of Manila. With supplies purchased from the United States Army and money borrowed from Elizalde and Company, the university built a new facility.

The charity unit opened on February 15, 1945 and the private, pay hospital of the University of Santo Tomas opened on March 7, 1946. The hospital subsequently grew, with the units combining with the completion of the University of Santo Tomas Hospital quadrangle in 1959.

The hospital was early in offering genetic counseling to patients in the Philippines, with a prenatal diagnostic clinic opening in 1984. It opened a pediatric intensive care unit three years thereafter.

Objectives and Quality Policy

OBJECTIVES

  • To monitor the implementation of high level health care standards through regular review of indices (i.e., infection rates, adverse events, mortality rates, etc.) and other pertinent measures or methods of assessments.
  • To ensure continuing improvement of hospital services and organizational efficiency, through leadership engagement, risk based thinking, corrective actions and conduct of regular internal audits.
  • To maintain the competencies of its health care staff through continuous professional education and workshops.
  • To maintain the national accreditation of the USTH Internship, Residency and Fellowship training Programs.
  • To maintain the established hospital quality management system through ISO 9001:2015 accreditation.
  • To maintain the highest level (Level III) of the Department of Health (DOH) Accreditation of the Hospital.
  • To maintain the highest level of PhilHealth Accreditation.
  • To maintain the accreditation of the Hospital by the Philippine Health Research Ethics Board (PHREB) and Forum for Ethical Review Committee in Asia and Western Pacific (FERCAP).
  • To retain existing local and international linkages and develop other partnerships and collaborations.
  • To continue to support the different Faculties and Colleges of the University of Santo Tomas in pursuit of knowledge in the field of medical science and technology particularly through training and research.

QUALITY POLICY

In pursuit of the USTH Vision and Mission, we commit ourselves to:

  • the consistent delivery of quality healthcare by adhering to the highest standards of professionalism;
  • the provision of our physicians, nurses, employees, students and other trainees with the necessary support to update their knowledge and improve their competence;
  • the continuous implementation and improvement of our quality management systems to assure their relevance to the advances of health care and technology in order to meet the needs of our clientele;
  • the safety and protection not only of our clients but also our allied health professionals and other stakeholders;
  • the development of an updated quality management system through good planning and management of resources
We shall comply with all applicable national and international statutory and regulatory requirements including those of the medical, nursing and allied health boards, societies and associations we subscribe to.
