Teraphobia

Teraphobia (fear of monsters) is extremely common in pre-school-age children. It generally lessens during the early elementary years and is highly uncommon by the time a child reaches middle school. In teens and adults, the fear of monsters is a rare but potentially life-limiting phobia.

  • Learn More

Causes of Teraphobia

In children, the fear of monsters often takes a nonspecific form. Rather than fearing Frankenstein, Dracula or Godzilla, the child is afraid that "a monster" lives under his bed or in her closet.

Causes of Teraphobia

Nonetheless, asking the child to draw a picture of the monster may provide clues to an environmental trigger. Some drawings resemble a TV cartoon character, a kidnapper who appeared on the evening news or even a neighbor whom the kids in the neighborhood refer to as "creepy."

Causes of Teraphobia

Older kids and adults may suffer from this phobia unnaturally, likely caused by watching scary movies about evil creatures.

Symptoms of Teraphobia

Like other mental disorders, if the sufferer begins to exhibit the following symptoms when presented with the source of fear, he or she may very well have tetraphobia.

Not wanting to go out at night

Persons with Teraphobia may experience fear of going out at night because they are afraid that there is a monsters hiding somewhere and staring at them every night to eat their flesh alive. Even it doesn't exist.

Can't sleep without lights on

A person with Teraphobia are afraid that their bedroom has a monster at night and can't sleep without turning their lights on to check if there is a monster even these monsters doesn't exist.

Insomnia

They experience trouble of falling and staying asleep because of too much anxiety and worried that a monster might kill or eat their flesh if they sleep at night.
