Ablutophobia

Ablutophobia is the overwhelming fear of bathing, cleaning, or washing. It’s an anxiety disorder that falls under the category of specific phobias.Specific phobias are irrational fears centered on a particular situation. They can disrupt your life. An estimated 19.2 million adults in the United States are affected by specific phobias. Specific phobias generally have common symptoms, causes, and treatments. Ablutophobia is more common in women and children than in men.

  • Learn More

Causes of Ablutophobia

Negative experience. You had some sort of traumatic experience that involved bathing or washing.

Causes of Ablutophobia

Your Genetics. You’re more likely to have ablutophobia if one of your parents had it.

Causes of Ablutophobia

Changes in brain functioning. This includes injury, aging, and more.

Symptoms of Ablutophobia

The symptoms of ablutophobia are the same as with most specific phobias. Symptoms may occur when faced with bathing or washing. They may also occur when just thinking about it. Intense fear or anxiety that increases the closer you get to the situation.
