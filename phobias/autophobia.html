Autophobia

Autophobia, or monophobia, is the fear of being alone or lonely. Being alone, even in a usually comforting place like home, can result in severe anxiety for people with this condition. People with autophobia feel they need another person or other people around in order to feel safe.

  • Learn More

Causes of Autophobia

Autophobia is an irrational anxiety that develops when a person fears they may end up alone. While there may not be an actual threat of being alone, the person will still be unable to control their symptoms.

Causes of Autophobia

It can be a result of a trauma in which you were harmed because you were alone and vulnerable, or you had no one to help you deal with a horrifying event.

Causes of Autophobia

It can also be part of a larger panic disorder or borderline personality disorder where an inability to self-soothe and fear of abandonment are additional factors at play.

Symptoms of Autophobia

A person will develop symptoms of the disorder when they get into a situation in which they could end up alone.

Obsessively worrying about being alone

They feel worried that being alone makes them feel unsafe. They can't walk alone without another person.

Experiencing fears of what could happen while being alone

People with Autophobia are afraid that something bad might happen if they walk alone. So, they need someone to be with them to overcome this anxiety.

Feeling detached from your body when alone

They would feel like their body would be separate whenever they feel alone.

Experiencing shaking, sweating, chest pain, dizziness, heart palpitations, hyperventilation, and nausea when alone or in a situation where you could soon become alone

Extreme anxiety would attack if they left alone. So, they need someone, better if someone that is close to them, in order for them to calm and feel comfortable with.

A feeling of extreme terror when alone or in a situation where you could soon become alone

They are afraid that something bad might happen if they walk alone in a place or in a event with no one else. So, they need someone to be with for them to keep calm and overcome this anxiety.
